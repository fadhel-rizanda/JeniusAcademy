Class Description:


In this course, students will embark on an exciting journey through the world of elementary mathematics. From mastering basic arithmetic operations like addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division to exploring fundamental concepts such as fractions, geometry, and measurement, students will build a strong foundation in mathematical thinking. Through engaging activities, interactive exercises, and real-world examples, Professor Parker will foster a love for math and develop students' problem-solving skills.


Learning Objectives:

  • Master basic arithmetic operations: addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division.
  • Understand fundamental concepts such as fractions, geometry, and measurement.
  • Develop problem-solving skills through real-world examples and interactive exercises.
  • Cultivate a love for mathematics and confidence in mathematical abilities.
  • Build a strong foundation for future mathematical learning and academic success.

Summary Of This Session:

Fundamentals of Elementary Mathematics

Welcome to your study guide for elementary mathematics! In our session, we covered several key topics to help you build a strong foundation in math. We started with basic arithmetic operations, including addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division. For example, addition involves combining numbers to get a total, such as 3 + 4 = 7, while subtraction finds the difference between numbers, like 10 - 4 = 6. We also explored multiplication, which totals a number repeated several times (e.g., 3 × 4 = 12), and division, which splits a number into equal parts (e.g., 12 ÷ 3 = 4).

Next, we delved into fundamental concepts such as fractions, which represent parts of a whole, like 1/2 (one-half) or 3/4 (three-quarters). We discussed geometry, focusing on shapes and their properties, including squares, circles, triangles, and rectangles. Measurement was another key topic, where we learned to determine the length, width, height, and volume of objects using units like inches, feet, centimeters, and liters.

To develop your problem-solving skills, we applied math to real-world examples and engaged in interactive exercises that made learning fun and practical. For studying at home, practice regularly by solving a few math problems each day. Use real-world examples, such as measuring ingredients for a recipe or calculating change when shopping, to apply math in everyday situations. Engage with interactive activities, including online games and apps focused on math practice, and review the key concepts to ensure you understand them.

Remember, the goal is to build a strong foundation in math while enjoying the learning process. Keep practicing, stay curious, and don't hesitate to ask for help if needed. Happy studying!

Excercise For This Session:

  1. Addition:

    • Sarah has 7 apples. She buys 5 more apples from the store. How many apples does she have now?
    • Tom had 13 marbles. His friend gave him 8 more marbles. How many marbles does Tom have in total?

  2. Subtraction:

    • Jake had 15 candies. He gave 7 candies to his sister. How many candies does Jake have left?
    • Emma had 20 pencils. She lost 4 of them. How many pencils does Emma have now?/li>

  3. Multiplication:

    • There are 6 baskets with 3 oranges in each basket. How many oranges are there in total?
    • A box contains 5 packs of markers, and each pack has 8 markers. How many markers are in the box?

  4. Division:

    • There are 18 cookies and 2 plates. If you divide the cookies equally between the plates, how many cookies will each plate have?
    • A teacher has 24 students and wants to divide them into groups of 6. How many groups will there be?
